--- layout: page title: About slug: about order: 1 --- I am a post-doc in Jua Gallego's group in the Department of Bioengineering at Imperial College London. Broadly, I am interested in the neural dynamics that support flexible behaviour. My current focus is in combining detailed investigation of rodent behaviour with large-scale neural recordings and contemporary population-level analyses to gain insights into cortical and subcortical computations during feedback-mediated motor control. Previously, I completed my PhD at Dartmouth in the lab of Matthijs van der Meer where I studied flexible processing of motivationally-relevant information in the rodent nucleus accumbens. Before Dartmouth, I started my research career with Bruce McKay at Wilfrid Laurier University where I investigated the effects of the abused solvent toluene on various aspects of neural function.
